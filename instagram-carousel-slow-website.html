Reason 1

Unoptimized
Images & Media

Massive, uncompressed images are the #1 cause of slow load times. Uploading a 5MB photo straight from your camera forces the browser to download heavy files before displaying anything.

The Fix: Compress images before uploading, use modern formats like WebP, and implement lazy loading so images only load when scrolled into view.
Swipe
Slide 03
Reason 2

Too Many
Plugins & Scripts

Every plugin, tracking code, and third-party script adds extra HTTP requests. If your site relies on 30+ plugins to function, it's carrying too much dead weight.

The Fix: Audit your plugins regularly. Delete what you don't use, combine scripts where possible, and consider custom-coded solutions instead of bloated off-the-shelf plugins.
Swipe
Slide 04
Reason 3

Poor Hosting &
No Caching

Cheap shared hosting means you're fighting for server resources with thousands of other sites. Without caching, your server has to rebuild the page from scratch for every single visitor.

The Fix: Invest in premium, dedicated hosting or a modern edge network (like Vercel/Netlify). Implement aggressive caching and use a CDN to serve content from servers closest to your users.

Confusing
Navigation & UX

If users can't figure out what you do or where to go within 3 seconds, they leave. A cluttered menu or unclear user journey creates friction that kills sales.

The Fix: Simplify your navigation. Guide users naturally from the hero section straight to your core offering with zero guesswork.

Reason 2

Weak
Call-to-Actions

Generic buttons like "Submit" or "Click Here" don't inspire action. If your CTAs blend into the background or lack clear value, users won't click them.

The Fix: Use action-oriented, benefit-driven CTAs. Instead of "Contact Us", try "Get Your Free Audit" or "Start Your Project". Make them visually pop.
